# Private Queries and NDA Policies

Need to talk to the primary maintainer of the Latios Framework about things you
don’t want others to know about?

We can make this happen, but know that I have boundaries, which this document
will outline.

## How to Reach Me in Realtime Chat as Fast as Possible

I make no promises I will be available the moment you reach out. But the
following has a \>50% chance of reaching me quickly.

Step 1: Join the [framework Discord server](https://discord.gg/DHraGRkA4n).

Step 2: Use the Discord server to find me and send a DM. I have the same
username on discord as in Unity Discussions.

Step 3: Post of message on the public Discord server to hint to me about the DM
request.

Note that I live in the United States far away from any coast, and am a bit of a
night owl.

## Other Ways to Reach Me

Discord is my preferred tool for any form of realtime communication. All other
forms of communication are typically only checked once or twice a day.

Unity Discussions has a personal message feature you can use to reach me. This
will notify me when I am at my framework development computer.

I also have a public email you can use. To find it, clone any of my
repositories, and use `git log -n 5` in the command line to see the email (I’m
trying to avoid giving this email to scraper bots). I try to check this once or
twice per day.

I have another private email address that I sometimes use for professional
services. If such a service has this email and has interest to reach me about
this framework or to inquire more details about statements I have made about the
service, I consent for the service to contact me via that email to discuss such
interests.

I do not mind asynchronous communication.

## Private Query Policies

For general queries about day-to-day framework usage and troubleshooting, I
generally prefer that those discussions happen on the framework discord so that
others can learn from the findings. However, if you strongly prefer to keep the
conversation private, I will respect that.

In general, I try to respect your work. If you send me a bug repro, I’m not
going to share that repro or take assets from it. I don’t have interest in
sharing the name of your project, what it is about, nor any tentative release
dates. If you feel the need to disclose some of that to solve a problem or to
provide feedback on the framework, I will do my best to respect that.

## NDA Policies

If you require a formal non-disclosure agreement in order to communicate, such a
thing is not out of the question, but there are a few precautions.

1.  If you don’t already know my full name, then we need to establish some kind
    of working relationship before I will disclose that information to sign an
    NDA.
2.  The NDA must be mutual.
3.  The agreement and the means of communication used must not disclose personal
    information (such as full names) to any third party unless that party is
    also under NDA and has specific interest in the framework.
4.  The NDA must not impede development of the framework in public because of
    risks of exposure of technical details learned from the communication under
    the NDA.
5.  The agreement does not stipulate any endorsement nor any required activity.

For (1 & 3), some people are reckless. And some are irrational. Several of my
future endeavors may include things that may trigger irrational people, and I
don’t want reckless people to enable irrational people to do irrational things
at my expense. Therefore, I don’t want to be added to a general-purpose NDA
group chat where my full name is exposed to a bunch of public influencer
insiders that have no interest in my framework.

Regarding (4), NDAs which are primarily intended to protect algorithms, designs,
and implementation details are a big no-no for me. If such things need to be
kept secret, then there should never be a reason for you to need to disclose
that to me.

Usually, the NDAs I am willing to sign are focused on names of projects,
statuses of projects (such as whether or not they exist or are being worked on),
timelines of projects, release dates of projects, and similar. If you are
working on an unannounced game and want to collaborate on a framework feature
using the game for validation and testing, an NDA makes a lot of sense. If you
are building an asset or service that the framework might need to adapt to, an
NDA can be used to protect distribution of design documents, conversations,
timelines, release targets, and other resources. But it cannot protect technical
details being added as comments in framework code (or made discoverable through
the code itself) to explain such an adaptation. And it cannot delay such
adaptive work.

People have ideas. Some ideas are good. Some are bad. Some will end up
succeeding. Some fail. And some require time to bake and evolve. I recognize
sharing too early is problematic for those who do not understand this concept.
But I do not want to restrain myself from sharing the lessons learned from these
experiences.

If you have any questions or are unsure what I am comfortable or not comfortable
protecting in an NDA, don’t hesitate to ask.
